## What Students Should Discover

By end of session, students should understand:

| Concept | Key Insight |
|---------|-------------|
| **2026 Reality** | AI agents ACT autonomously, humans supervise |
| **Role Shift** | From operator (writes prompts) to architect (designs systems) |
| **Multi-Agent** | Multiple specialized agents coordinating is the norm |
| **Governance** | Without structure, there's no scaling |
| **KAF** | Enterprise AI requires framework, not just demos |

### The "Aha" Moments

Look for students realizing:
- "Oh, the agent makes decisions, not just generates text"
- "Multiple agents can work together without human prompting each one"
- "The hard part isn't building agents, it's controlling them"
- "Governance isn't bureaucracy - it's what makes AI enterprise-ready"

## Intervention Triggers

### When to Step In

| Signal | Intervention |
|--------|--------------|
| Student stuck during AI Tutor | Ask: "What question did you ask the Tutor?" |
| Going too technical | Ask: "Would your customer understand that?" |
| Copying AI verbatim | Ask: "What's YOUR perspective on this?" |
| Finished too fast | Challenge: "What would a skeptical CISO ask?" |
| Group A (Chaos) too frustrated | Reassure: "That's the point - chaos without structure" |

### When NOT to Step In

- Student struggling but making progress with AI Tutor
- Healthy debate between participants
- Student refining their thinking
- Group A discovering chaos organically

## Common Questions & Answers

### "Is this going to take our jobs?"

> "AI changes jobs more than it eliminates them. But the jobs that remain require you to understand AI. That's why you're here."

### "Which AI model is best?"

> "It depends. GPT-5.2 for reasoning, Claude 4.5 for agentic tasks, Gemini 3 for long context. We'll explore this throughout the academy."

### "Why do we need governance? Can't we just build?"

> "You saw what happened in Part 4. That was 5 minutes with text. Imagine 8 agents running for 3 months. Governance is what makes AI enterprise-ready."

### "What about open source models?"

> "Llama 4 is now at parity with closed models for many use cases. We'll cover when to use what in the role tracks."

## Assessment Notes

### What to Look For in Agent Assignments

| Excellent | Good | Developing |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Clear single purpose | Mostly focused | Too broad |
| Explicit boundaries | Some boundaries | No "does NOT do" |
| Consistent output format | Generally consistent | Unpredictable |
| Realistic for their work | Plausible | Too ambitious/vague |
| Shows original thinking | Some originality | Generic agent idea |

### Red Flags

- Agent tries to do everything
- No boundaries defined
- Output examples are copy-paste from AI
- Doesn't relate to their actual work
- Over-engineered for Day 1
