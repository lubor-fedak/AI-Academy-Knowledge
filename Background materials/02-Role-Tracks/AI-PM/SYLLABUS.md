# AI Product Manager (AI-PM) Track Syllabus

## Role Overview

The AI Product Manager translates **business needs into AI solutions**, owns prioritization, and ensures the team delivers value. AI-PMs are the voice of the customer within the team.

This track represents a product role that partners closely with the Forward Deployed Engineer capability and its specializations.

### Core Responsibilities
- Frame AI use cases in business terms
- Prioritize features based on value and feasibility
- Manage stakeholder expectations
- Define success metrics and MVP scope

### Collaboration Points
| With | You Provide | You Receive |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| FDE | Requirements, priorities | Technical feasibility |
| AI-SE | Acceptance criteria | Estimates, constraints |
| AI-DS | Success metrics | Model capabilities |
| Client | Roadmap, expectations | Business needs |

## Key Situations

### S-PM-01: "Scope is Too Big" (Day 6)

```yaml
Context: |
  Client wants an AI that does 15 things.
  Sales promised delivery in 4 weeks.
  You have 3 engineers.
  Something has to give.
  
Challenge: |
  - Identify highest-value features
  - Define MVP vs. future phases
  - Communicate trade-offs clearly
```

### S-PM-02: "Prove the ROI" (Day 7)

```yaml
Context: |
  CFO asks: "How do we know this AI is worth $2M?"
  You need to build a business case.
  
Challenge: |
  - Quantify value (time, cost, revenue)
  - Address risks and assumptions
  - Create compelling narrative
```

## AI Tutor Focus Areas

### Primary Challenge Questions
- "What's the ROI?"
- "Who is the decision maker?"
- "What's NOT in the MVP?"
- "How do you know you've succeeded?"

### Red Flags
- Features without business justification
- Ignoring stakeholder concerns
- Unclear success criteria
- Scope without constraints
